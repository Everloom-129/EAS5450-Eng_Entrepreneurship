# Lec 27: Leader, Manager or more...?

04/29/2025

> *From Professor Zaleznik’s perspective in Managers and Leaders, do you see yourself as being a leader, a manager or…? Explain.*

Choosing between "Managers and Leaders," I see myself mostly as a leader rather than merely a manager. Zaleznik describes leaders as individuals who embrace uncertainty, creativity, and vision, while managers prefer stability and control. Reflecting on my experiences and values, I align more with the traits of a leader. 



Throughout my time as a MSROBO student at UPenn, I have consistently been driven by a pursuit of vision, imagination, and the desire to change the world around us. Leaders tolerate ambiguity and disorder, willing to delay closure to fully understand complex issues. This is very similar reinforcement learning, where I tried infinite time and countless models for drone racing and active perception tasks using franka robot arm. In these projects, both the robot agent and I learned to explore the uncertainty, trying to generate a creative solutions for the given environment. And tolerating uncertainty is a must to achieve really interesting outcomes.



Meanwhile, I was the founder and president of the PhiloCoffee Club at Zhejiang University. Doing a club at senior year is not just for fun, but also a practice to do start-up: you need choose right topic to sell, organize intelligent and helpful folks, deliver great experience to the public. This requires creativity, community-building, and inspiring others through philosophical and cultural discussions.  Additionally, within my lab and among my peers, I naturally take on a guiding role, often navigating team dynamics, resolving conflicts, and motivating collaborative efforts toward ambitious goals.



My experiences are closely reflected in the ideas presented by Austin and Nolan in "Bridging the Gap Between Stewards and Creators." They note significant conflicts between the practical, bottom-line-focused stewards (managers) and the creative, vision-driven creators (leaders).   My ambition to establish my own startup in the future, following the footsteps of my PhD mentor, further underscores my entrepreneurial leadership qualities. Inspired by Schumpeterian leadership, I am driven by the excitement and passion for creating something new, embracing risks, and continuously innovating.



Moreover, the reflection on Schumpeterian leadership underlines entrepreneurship as a critical leadership quality—characterized by imagination, risk-taking, and a desire to innovate. I totally agree Schumpeter’s notion of entrepreneurial leaders driven by "the joy of creating" and a commitment to pursuing novel ideas despite the potential risks and high uncertainty. GRASP Lab, Venture Lab, I am always ready to commit in the first round, doing great things with great people



In conclusion, considering Zaleznik’s definitions and my personal experiences in academics, entrepreneurship, and community leadership, I clearly identify as a leader, driven by vision, creativity, and the desire to make meaningful changes in my field and beyond.

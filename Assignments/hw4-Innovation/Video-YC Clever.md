Tyler Bosmeny's talk on sales and marketing from $0-1 million provides insights into the early sales process of a startup, particularly for those with a technical background. Here's a breakdown of key points based on the provided sources and your query:

- **Best Salesperson in the Early Days:** In the early stages of a startup, the founder is the best salesperson. This is because founders possess a unique passion for the product and in-depth industry knowledge, which often outweighs formal sales experience. It is also very common that the founder will be the one doing the early sales.

- Sales Funnel:

   The sales funnel consists of different stages:

  - **Prospecting**: Identifying potential customers.
  - **Conversations**: Engaging with interested prospects.
  - **Closing**: Converting prospects into paying customers and signing the deal.
  - **Revenue**: The ultimate goal of generating income.

- **Diffusion of Innovation Curve:** When a tech company is new and risky, its customers are typically the **"innovators"**. These are the 2.5% of the market that are willing to try new things. This implies that a startup will need to do a lot of outreach to get early sales.

- Reaching Prospective Customers:

  - **Personal Network**: Utilize existing connections.
  - **Conferences**: Attend industry-specific events where your target audience gathers. Get attendee lists in advance, and email every single person to set up meetings.
  - **Cold Email**: Send concise emails to potential customers.  A short template includes who you are, what you're building, and an invitation to talk.

- **Key Characteristic of a Successful Sales Conversation:** The most important thing is **to listen more than you talk**. The salesperson should aim to understand the customer's needs and problems, potentially even better than the customer does. The goal is to spend only 30% of the call talking and 70% of the call letting the customer speak.

- Evolving Sales Approach:

   As a company grows towards $100 million in annual sales, the sales approach must change:

  - **Initial Unscalable Approach:** In the beginning, founders do not need to think about scaling. It's okay to do things that don't scale to get early customers.
  - **Scalable Methods:** Startups must consider the deal size and how that affects the sales process. A company selling a $100,000 product will have a high touch sales cycle, whereas a company selling a product for $1,000 needs a more scalable approach. If you are selling a low cost product, and your sales process involves flying out to see them, that will not scale. Companies often fall into one of the following buckets: a few customers buying a very expensive product, many customers buying a moderately priced product, or a huge number of customers buying a low priced product.

*   **What is a "priced round" of venture financing?**
    A priced round of venture financing is when a specific valuation is assigned to the startup. In this type of round, an investor pays $X$ for $Y\%$ of the startup, which sets the startup’s post-money valuation at $X/Y\%.$

*   **What is a convertible note? What are the fundamental differences between a convertible note and a priced round?**
    A convertible note is an unpriced security that provides a startup with $X without determining a specific fraction of the startup's equity. It delays valuation and pricing until the next round of financing.
    *   **Key Differences**:/‘**Valuation**: Convertible notes do not assign an immediate valuation to the company, whereas priced rounds do.
        *   **Timing**: Convertible notes delay valuation until a later financing round.
        *   **Equity**: In a priced round, the investor gets equity immediately. With a convertible note, the investor's money converts into equity at a later date, usually with a discount.
    
*   **What are the differences between a convertible note and a Simple Agreement for Future Equity (SAFE)?**
    *   **Instrument Type**: A convertible note is a debt instrument, while a SAFE is an equity instrument.
    *   **Maturity Date**: Convertible notes have a maturity date, which is the date the loan is due. SAFEs do not have a maturity date.
    *   **Interest**: Convertible notes typically have an interest component, whereas SAFEs do not.
    *   **Liquidation Preference**: Convertible notes typically issue the same security to seed investors as to Series A investors, resulting in a liquidation preference exceeding the initial investment. SAFEs account for this by creating a new security at the time of Series A financing where the liquidation preference equals the face value of the initial investment.

*   **What is the difference between a pre-money SAFE and a post-money SAFE?**
    The key difference between pre-money and post-money SAFEs lies in when the equity conversion is calculated upon an equity financing. A post-money SAFE includes the conversion of the SAFEs in its calculation, while a pre-money SAFE does not. The post-money SAFE provides more clarity regarding an investor's ultimate ownership because each SAFE's conversion calculation is independent.

*   **Why is a convertible note advantageous to an investor? A SAFE?**
    *   **Convertible Note Advantages for Investors**:
        *   **Deferral of Pricing**: Investors can delay complex pricing issues until a later date, leveraging the expertise of more sophisticated investors like VCs in subsequent rounds.
        *   **Discount on Equity**: They typically convert into equity at a discount to the next round's price, rewarding them for early investment risk.
        *   **Valuation Cap**: Convertible notes with caps ensure a minimum ownership percentage upon conversion, compensating for the risk taken in the seed round.
    *   **SAFE Advantages for Investors**:
        *   **Simplicity**: SAFEs are designed to be simpler and more streamlined than convertible notes, allowing for quicker alignment on investment terms.
        *   **Early-Stage Incentives**: Like convertible notes, SAFEs provide investors with incentives such as discount rates and valuation caps as a reward for early investment.

*   **Why is a convertible note advantageous to an entrepreneur? A SAFE?**
    *   **Convertible Note Advantages for Entrepreneurs**:
        *   **Faster Funding**: Convertible notes allow entrepreneurs to raise money faster and at a lower cost compared to traditional financing. Legal fees are lower, and the financing can close more quickly.
        *   **Ability to Increase Valuation**: By delaying pricing, entrepreneurs can increase their valuation before the next financing round, retaining a greater ownership share.
        *   **Tailored Pricing**: Discounts and caps allow entrepreneurs to tailor the pricing of the round to different investors.
    *   **SAFE Advantages for Entrepreneurs**:
        *   **No Maturity Date**: SAFEs do not have a maturity date, preventing potential insolvency issues if a qualified financing round does not occur in time.
        *   **No Interest Payments**: SAFEs do not accrue interest, reducing the financial burden on the startup.

*   **What is the rationale for a "discount" in a convertible note? How does it work?**
    The rationale for a discount in a convertible note is to reward investors for taking on the high risk of investing in an early-stage startup. The discount gives the seed investor more shares upon conversion than they would have received if they had invested in the next round. For example, a 20% discount means the convertible note holder gets the stock at 80% of the price paid by new investors in the next equity financing.

*   **What is the nature of the divergent interests of the traditional convertible note investor and the entrepreneur in the period prior to the Series A investment?**
    The entrepreneur has an incentive to use the money from the seed round to increase the startup’s valuation to more than compensate for the discount. The investor's advice or help to the startup can ironically dilute the investor more if it leads to a higher valuation, which is not in the investor's best interest.

*   **What is the rationale for a "valuation cap" in a convertible note or SAFE? How does it work? In what circumstances is a cap favorable for the investor, and in what circumstances is it favorable for the entrepreneur?**
    *   **Rationale**: A valuation cap protects seed investors from excessive dilution if the company's valuation increases significantly in the next financing round. It sets a maximum valuation at which the convertible note or SAFE will convert into equity.
    *   **How it Works**: The convertible note converts at the lower of (1) a discount to the next round’s valuation or (2) the valuation cap.
    *   **Favorable for the Investor**: A valuation cap is favorable for the investor when the company’s valuation at the next financing round is very high. The cap ensures the investor receives a larger equity stake than they would have if only the discount applied.
    *   **Favorable for the Entrepreneur**: A higher valuation cap is generally more favorable for the entrepreneur, as it reduces dilution. However, setting a low cap can incentivize investors to help grow the company's value.

*   **What additional terms are typically included in a convertible note transaction?**
    Additional terms typically included in a convertible note transaction:
    *   **Duration of the note**: This specifies the term length of the note.
    *   **Interest rate**: The interest rate paid on the note.
    *   **Discount when converting**: The percentage discount applied when the note converts into equity.
    *   **Valuation cap**: A maximum valuation at which the note converts.
    *   **Board seats**: Whether the investor receives a seat on the board of directors.
    *   **Acquisition**: What happens to the note in the event of an acquisition.
    *   **No financing event**: What happens if no financing takes place during the note's duration.

# CEO of Novoloop
Jie Wang
04/09/2025



*Today’s guest speaker is Miranda Wang, co-founder and CEO of Novoloop, an emerging leader in plastic*
*recycling, turning post-consumer plastic waste into high-performance materials that can be used in a*
*variety of products. Founded in 2015 and headquartered in Menlo Park, California, the venture-backed*
*startup is building and scaling new technologies to greatly reduce both the amount of plastic that enters*
*landfills every year and the amount of CO2 gas emissions from the production of plastics materials. With a*
*vision to end the plastic catastrophe, Novoloop is transforming waste into applications for footwear,*
*sporting goods, and automotive industries. In 2022, Novoloop was recognized in the World Economic*
*Forum’s 100 most promising Technology Pioneers. In February 2022, their $11M Series A raise was*
*oversubscribed and increased to \$21M that June. To date they have raised more than \$50M from venture*
*capital, US governmental grants, and philanthropic foundations.*
*Construction of Novoloop’s pilot plant was completed in Surat, India, in March 2024 and has achieved*
*continuous operations with the usage of commercially representative plastic waste feedstock. This*
*milestone marks the successful transition from batch reactions to an integrated and automated plant that*
*validates Novoloop's first-of-a-kind Lifecycling™ technology.*
*Miranda has won several awards including selection to Forbes 30 Under 30 social entrepreneurs list, the*
*UN Environment Programme’s “Young Champion of the Earth” award, the Rolex Award for Enterprise,*
*the Pritzker Environmental "Genius" Award, TIME magazine’s “Women Who Will Save The World” and*
*Entrepreneur magazine’s “100 Women of Influence.”*
*Miranda graduated from the University of Pennsylvania College of Arts & Sciences with a BA in Biology*
*and an Engineering Entrepreneurship minor. Miranda started Biocellection, Novoloop’s predecessor,*
*while at Penn and won all five major awards of the Wharton Startup Challenge, including being the first*
*undergraduate ever to win the Perelman Grand Prize.*

## Questions

1. Novoloop operates at the intersection of multiple fields — chemical engineering, sustainability, and advanced materials. If you had to define Novoloop with a single label, what would it be and why? How does that framing influence your strategy and communication with stakeholders?

2. Beyond the public benefit of sustainability, what is Novoloop’s core business value? How do you position your products competitively against traditional material providers, especially given the potentially higher costs of advanced recycled materials?

3. What motivated you to launch your startup in the United States rather than in Canada? How did the U.S. innovation ecosystem shape your early decisions and growth trajectory?

4. Could you share more about the behind-the-scenes negotiations between Novoloop and Worley regarding the preliminary engineering study for the 20,000-ton plant? What was the most challenging part of that collaboration — and was there anything that surprised you in the process that hasn’t been publicly shared?

5. After pandemic and the explosive rise of GenAI, attention to green technology seems to have declined in public discourse and investment. From your perspective, how has this shift impacted Novoloop and the broader climate tech space? Do you see any new paradigms emerging from this transition?

1. Given your background in Materials Science & Engineering and Engineering Entrepreneurship, how did your technical and entrepreneurial education at Penn specifically prepare you for startup? Especially, what drove you from working in data analytics at Blackstone to co-founding Pickle, a fashion marketplace?



2. Pickle's model includes taking a 20% cut from each transaction. How did you arrive at this specific revenue model, and how have user behaviors or competitive dynamics influenced your approach to pricing and monetization strategies over time?



3. Given China's highly competitive e-commerce ecosystem, with platforms like Xiaohongshu (Rednote) and Xianyu dominating the peer-to-peer and fashion-sharing markets, what do you perceive as the primary challenges and opportunities if Pickle were to enter China? How would you differentiate Pickle in such a saturated market?



4. China's e-commerce giants (such as Shein and Temu) leverage advanced AI-driven recommendations, automated inventory management, and customer interaction tools. What's your perspective on the role AI and automation could play in Pickle’s future growth, and are there particular AI-driven initiatives or technologies you've already begun implementing or exploring?



5. From your experience transitioning from a traditional corporate environment (such as Blackstone) to the entrepreneurial world, what notable differences have you observed in consumer behaviors or adoption of peer-to-peer marketplaces between U.S. and Chinese customers? How might these differences influence your global expansion strategy?

   

6. With increasing competition from low-cost, highly efficient Chinese e-commerce platforms entering international markets, how do you balance profitability with maintaining a premium user experience at Pickle, especially regarding pricing strategies, customer retention, and operational efficiencies?

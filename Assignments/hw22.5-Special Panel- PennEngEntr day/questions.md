# Penn Eng Entrepreneurship Session

04/10/2025



1. For frontier areas like AI-powered robotics, where both technical uncertainty and capital intensity are high, how do you think about the appropriate financing structure—venture equity versus venture debt—especially at early stages?

2. As embodied AI becomes more integrated into real-world environments—from homes to hospitals—how do you assess the unique risks of these startups compared to traditional software or infrastructure plays? Are investors ready for that kind of exposure?

3. One common confusion about robotics is that, If a team is working on cutting-edge technology that isn’t fully productized yet,  what carries the most weight in your evaluation—team, technical vision, or early signs of market alignment?

4. As someone joining a robotics startup fresh out of school, how would you recommend I begin building meaningful relationships with investors and financial institutions—beyond just raising funding?”

5. Do you see embodied AI or robotics becoming a more geopolitically sensitive area like semiconductors or cybersecurity? And how should young founders think about regulatory risk when committing to these paths?”
